Recommended core building blocks

For global and US exposure, the domicile-clean route is an Irish-domiciled UCITS fund (listed in London): no US estate-tax exposure and 15% — not 30% — dividend withholding. For Singapore, Asia, income and thematics, SGX-listed funds are efficient. Start here, then explore the full list below.

The three costs of owning an ETF

Two of these are invisible on any price chart. A cheap headline fee means little if the fund's domicile quietly taxes your dividends and your estate.
1 · Expense ratio (TER)
The fund's annual management fee, taken from returns daily. Broad index ETFs run 0.05–0.30%; niche/thematic funds 0.45–0.80%.
2 · Dividend withholding
Tax withheld on dividends inside the fund. A US-domiciled fund suffers 30% on US dividends for a Singaporean; an Irish UCITS suffers 15% (treaty). Permanent drag.
3 · US estate tax
On death, US-situs assets above US$60k are taxed up to 40% before heirs inherit. Follows the asset, not your broker. Avoided entirely with non-US-domiciled funds.

Forward-looking return, not last year's

Good choices rest on expected long-run return, not the trailing twelve months. Recent winners often carry the lowest forward return — a reason to diversify and rebalance, not to chase. Figures are synthesised house estimates (2025-26 vintage); treat them as directional, not precise.
